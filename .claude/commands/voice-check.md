# /voice-check — Voice calibration

Calibrate the prose against the touchstones: Marilynne Robinson, John Berger, Donella Meadows.

For: **$ARGUMENTS**

(Example: "drafts/ch08_makers_dilemma.md", or "the last three paragraphs of preface")

## Process

1. **Read** the specified passage of our draft.

2. **Pull up reference passages** via web_search:
   - Three paragraphs of Marilynne Robinson from any essay (try *The Givenness of Things* or *The Death of Adam*).
   - Three paragraphs of John Berger from any essay (try *Ways of Seeing* or *About Looking*).
   - Two or three paragraphs of Donella Meadows from *Thinking in Systems* or her "Leverage Points" essay.

3. **Read all four** (our draft and the three references). Honestly assess:
   - **Cadence.** Does ours have the unhurried rhythm theirs do? Or is it pushing?
   - **Specificity.** Are our examples as concrete as theirs?
   - **Trust.** Do they trust the reader to follow without hand-holding? Are we?
   - **Confidence vs. performance.** Theirs is calm. Is ours?
   - **Sentence-length variation.** Theirs varies. Does ours?

4. **Identify the gap.** In two or three sentences, name what is missing in our draft that theirs has. Be specific.

5. **Revise** the passage to close the gap. Save in place.

6. **Commit:** `drafts: voice calibration pass on [section]`

## Report at the end

- The specific gap you identified.
- The two or three changes you made to close it.
- Whether the passage is now closer to the touchstones — and where the remaining distance lies.
